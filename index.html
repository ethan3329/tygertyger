Script:

Imagine that you have studied the ongoing extinction of tigers in the wild for the past century. You also learn that in 2010 there were just 3,200 tigers in the wild, compared to around 100,000 decades earlier. What happened to the tigers? While that may be a rhetorical question, we will explain to you why the population of the tiger is becoming gravely endangered. Picture this, you see a tiger cub that is younger than a week old. In many instances, the cub may not survive to full maturity because of evil poachers who prey upon them for financial gain, with a goal to sell the tigers’ fur to make clothing and accessories. Another reason for the decrease in the tiger population is due to habitat loss and prey depletion. Specifically, the land upon which they survive is being overrun by humans and thereby reducing the area the tigers need to find prey to survive. 

Today, there are fewer than 4,000 wild tigers in Asia. I am January Jones, and I would like to propose a solution. Let’s stop killing these beautiful creatures, purses, bags, and shoes.  We solicit your support to stand with us and help restore the tiger population. We can achieve this through our adoption program that will help relocate these tigers outside of the reach and range of poachers.

Information speech

 The facts do not lie. From 1900 until 2021, the tiger population has decreased drastically from 100,000 to fewer than 4,000 tigers left in the wild of Asia, thus putting them on the brink of extinction. In Indonesia, the law states that anyone caught hunting the tigers will be fined and sentenced to jail. However, this law does not stop the deforestation of their habitats, which is a big part of the decreasing population of tigers. A fully grown tiger could weigh up to 600 pounds, depending on the species. To survive, a tiger needs to eat approximately 6,600 pounds of living prey each year. This is why the loss of habitat is devastating to their survival. As stated on our partner’s website, the World Wildlife Foundation, “Accelerating deforestation and rampant poaching mean this noble creature could end up extinct like its Javan and Balinese counterparts.” This shows that if we do not help, once again more species will certainly become extinct.
